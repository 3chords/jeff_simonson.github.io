Of the four scatter plots, the only chart where a relationship exists with latitude is temperature.

The inverted U shape of the data suggests as you get closer to zero (the equator) the temperature tends to rise. And as you work closer to the poles (+/- 60 degrees latitude) the temperatures drop. In the northern hemisphere the temperatures stay elevated around the 20 degrees north mark but there is visual separation around 30 degrees where temperatures drop off. The same separation for the southern hemisphere occurs around the -25 degrees (south) mark.
